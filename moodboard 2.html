Patterns & motifs

Plaid, not just accent — the crosshatch running behind this whole page — now a base layer, not a border trim.
Checkerboard and gingham tile — the two-tone grid from patchwork floors and nail art — a natural fit for tables, trackers, and calendar cells.
Etched silver and brass — armor-style engraved metal alongside the warmer brass gold, so cool and warm metallics both get a seat.
Collage-page layering — photos, color blocks, and text stacked and overlapping rather than sitting in tidy separate boxes.
Turned-wood, scalloped silhouettes — mixed-era furniture shapes as inspiration for card edges, dividers, and corners.
No empty space — gaps between sections get a color block instead of being left as blank page — the new rule alongside "mesh, don't match."
Worn-in over polished — hand-painted signs, stitched leather, imperfect texture — favored over anything flat or corporate-clean.
Mesh, don't match — the core rule, in your own words: combine colors, patterns, and eras on purpose instead of keeping any one thing uniform.
