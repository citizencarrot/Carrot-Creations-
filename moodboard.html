Living style reference

Eclectic Maximalist Jewel & Pastel

Deep jewel tones now sit next to soft pastels — not one or the other. Pulled from the nail art, vintage furniture finds, and the Y2K/early-web collage pages you've been sending, this palette leans into that scrapbook energy: rich saturated color blocked right up against something soft and candy-colored. Nothing here is meant to match — it's meant to mesh.

Patterns & motifs

Stripe and dot fields — bold, graphic, unapologetic — not a floral accent but a full background player. Works as chart series, dividers, or section backdrops.
Checkerboard and gingham tile — the two-tone grid from patchwork floors and nail art — a natural fit for tables, trackers, and calendar cells.
Etched silver and brass — armor-style engraved metal alongside the warmer brass gold, so cool and warm metallics both get a seat.
Collage-page layering — pulled straight from the Y2K/early-web references: photos, color blocks, and text stacked and overlapping rather than sitting in tidy separate boxes.
Turned-wood, scalloped silhouettes — mixed-era furniture shapes as inspiration for card edges, dividers, and corners.
Saturated-against-soft blocking — a jewel tone or a pastel gets its own solid block right next to a contrasting neutral — high contrast, no gradients smoothing it over.
Worn-in over polished — hand-painted signs, stitched leather, imperfect texture — favored over anything flat or corporate-clean.
Mesh, don't match — the core rule, in your own words: combine colors, patterns, and eras on purpose instead of keeping any one thing uniform.
