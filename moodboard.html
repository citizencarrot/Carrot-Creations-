Patterns & motifs

Stripes & polka dots as core patterns — bold graphic stripes and dot fields (not just floral accents) show up constantly — strong candidates for chart series, dividers, and backgrounds.
Gingham & checkerboard tile — patchwork/checker floor tiles in mixed colors — great model for grid layouts, table striping, or calendar/tracker cells.
Engraved silver metal — armor-inspired ornate metallic surfaces (etched, riveted, polished) — good for icon treatments, borders, or a metallic accent finish alongside the gold.
Mixed-era furniture silhouettes — turned wood legs, scalloped table edges, layered shelving — good inspiration for card shapes, dividers, or corner treatments.
Color-blocked contrast — one bold saturated color against a warm neutral or contrasting wall — good model for primary/background pairing in UI.
Worn-in, handmade texture — vintage leather, hand-painted signs, stitched details — leans toward texture and imperfection over flat, corporate polish.
Mesh, don't match — your own words: deliberately combining colors/patterns/textures rather than keeping things uniform — this is the core design principle, not just a palette.
